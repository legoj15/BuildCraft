<lore>
Sometimes, you just need to erect something quick and dirty, you don't care what is made out of in the end, you just want that shape, and all you need is a template of sorts... 
</lore>
<no_lore>
A blank Template, ready to be filled by an Architect Table. Stacks to 16. Records only which positions in the captured volume are occupied. Scans roughly three times faster than a Blueprint and takes up less file size.
</no_lore>
<chapter name="Information"/>
Drop a Clean Template into the input slot of an <link inline="buildcraftunofficial:block/architect"/> bound to a Land Mark volume; when the scan finishes, the slot empties and a filled Template appears in the output slot. The filled item is single-stack and carries the name set in the Architect's GUI before scanning. The tooltip shows that name; F3+H advanced tooltips additionally show the snapshot's content hash, creation date, and owner UUID, with a rotating 3D preview drawn beneath on hover.
Feed the filled Template into a <link inline="buildcraftunofficial:block/builder"/> together with a stack of whichever block you want the shape stamped out in — the Builder copies that block into every captured position. Templates are the right choice for repetitive structural work where the material can vary: walls, floors, hollow shells, platform footings, the perimeter of a quarry pit.
For a snapshot that records the original block states (and entities) instead of just the shape, see <link inline="buildcraftunofficial:item/blueprint_clean"/>.
