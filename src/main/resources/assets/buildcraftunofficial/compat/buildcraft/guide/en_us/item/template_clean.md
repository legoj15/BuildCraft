<lore>
Sometimes, you just need to erect something quick and dirty, you don't care what is made out of in the end, you just want that shape, and all you need is a template of sorts... 
</lore>
<no_lore>
A blank Template, ready to be filled by an Architect Table. Stacks to 16. Records only which positions in the captured volume are occupied. Scans roughly three times faster than a Blueprint and takes up less file size.
</no_lore>
<chapter name="Information"/>
Insert a Template into the input slot of an <link inline="buildcraftunofficial:block/architect"/> bound to a Land Mark volume When the scan finishes, the input blueprint is used (whether it is clean or already used) and is placed into the output slot with the new data written to it. The blueprint will show the name and a 3D snapshot of the captured structure in its tooltip.
To deploy the saved structure, slot the blueprint into a <link inline="buildcraftunofficial:block/builder"/> and supply it with the same resources that were used at the time of the blueprint's capture.
If building every individual block 1:1 while even keeping the contents of chests, see <link inline="buildcraftunofficial:item/blueprint_clean"/>.
